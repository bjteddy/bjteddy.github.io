Aircraft LLP Tracking System

*** For confidentiality reasons, the full explanation behind this project cannot be disclosed.




role

During my time at Bombardier Aerospace, I was responsible for managing an aircraft assembly project for business jet Global 5000/6000. I managed a team of 4 people, and I got to work with aviation engineers, methods agents and quality inspectors.




context

A Life Limited Part (LLP) is an aircraft part which has a pre-established life expectancy value. This value is measured by either hours of use or cycles of flight. A life expectancy value indicates a Life Limited Part’s absolute maximum period of serviceability; a Life Limited Part must be dismounted from an aircraft before its life expectancy is due. This procedure is strictly enforced by Canadian Aviation Regulations. Tracking Life Limited Parts internally within Bombardier ensures the company to use unexpired parts during the aircraft assembly process. Tracking Life Limited Parts also allows the company to collect and conserve the parts’ metadata internally, which can be delivered to aircraft buyers upon request.




problem

LLP’s traditionally had not been systematically tracked. This lack of tracking becomes problematic if and when a spanned LLP (an LLP with hours/cycles logged to it) is unidentifiable from an un-spanned one. Untracked LLP’s can potentially lead to safety liability and subsequently to business depreciation. For example, a spanned LLP may unknowingly be assembled into a certified brand-new aircraft due to confusion in LLP inventory control. This results in having to re-inspect, re-assemble and re-certify the aircraft of concern, which are all subjected to cost and unnecessary expenditures on Bombardier’s behalf. More importantly, such an incidence may lead to loss of customer trust. Clients may question the quality, safety and engineering integrity of Bombardier aircrafts. Loss of customer trust can negatively impact aircraft sales and ultimately lead to business loss.
